## 🏛️ History Page [HistoryPage](cite: 1)

### Hero[cite: 1]

- **Title:** 120 Years of *History*[cite: 1]
- **Subtitle:** 120 years of neoclassical history, academic excellence, and cultural transformation in the heart of Galata.[cite: 1]
- **Badge:** 1885 — Year of Foundation[cite: 1]

### History Section[cite: 1]

- **Title:** History[cite: 1]
- **Paragraph 1:** The Galata Greek School is one of the important structures among the educational institutions realized by the Greek community during the modernization process of the Ottoman Empire.[cite: 1] The school was built on land donated by the Galata Greek Community, with the support of Eleni Zarifi, a philanthropist of the time, in order to contribute to the modern education and teaching of Greek children.[cite: 1]
- **Paragraph 2:** The architects of the school are Patroklos Kambanakis and Stavros Hristidis; Perikli Fotiadis provided consulting architectural services.[cite: 1] The opening of the school took place on Wednesday, June 2, 1910, at 11:00 AM.[cite: 1]

### Chronology [Timeline](cite: 1)

| Year[cite: 1]  | Title[cite: 1]                                    | Description[cite: 1] |
|------|-------------------------------------------|----------|
| 1885[cite: 1] | Foundation[cite: 1]                                   | Galata Greek School was founded.[cite: 1] |
| 1910[cite: 1] | Official Opening[cite: 1]                              | The official opening took place on Wednesday, June 2, 1910, at 11:00 AM.[cite: 1] |
| 1958[cite: 1] | Facade Arrangement[cite: 1]                         | The facade elevation of the building was arranged during the expansion of the main street.[cite: 1] |
| 1988[cite: 1] | Suspension of Educational Activities[cite: 1]    | Educational activities were suspended due to demographic changes.[cite: 1] |
| 2000[cite: 1] | Kindergarten Era[cite: 1]                           | The building served as a kindergarten between 2000 and 2007.[cite: 1] |
| 2012[cite: 1] | Return[cite: 1]                                      | The building was returned to the Istanbul Greek Community.[cite: 1] |
| 2015[cite: 1] | End of School Function[cite: 1]                | The building officially ceased to function as a school.[cite: 1] |
| 2019[cite: 1] | Start of Restoration[cite: 1]                    | A major restoration project was initiated to transform it into a cultural and educational center.[cite: 1] |

### Middle Block[cite: 1]

- **Title:** Built in the neoclassical-eclectic architectural style of the period, the school is located in Galata, the historical trade and financial center of Istanbul, at the intersection of the Kemeraltı and Bereketzade neighborhoods.[cite: 1]
- **Subtitle:** The school building, which resonated with the voices of children in the past but remained silent for many years, was returned to its owners in 2012.[cite: 1]

### Restoration Section[cite: 1]

- **Title:** Restoration[cite: 1]
- **Paragraph 1:** The implementation of the restoration project was initiated under the auspices of His All-Holiness Ecumenical Patriarch Bartholomew I.[cite: 1] It was completed thanks to the generous and elegant sponsorships of Marina and Athanasios Martinos.[cite: 1] The restoration was carried out under the leadership of Murat Tabanlıoğlu (Tabanlıoğlu Architects).[cite: 1]
- **Paragraph 2:** The Galata Greek School, with its new institutional name "The School," aims to be both an institution of Greek identity and cultural heritage by preserving its memory and deep-rooted past, and an art and education space that embraces all Istanbulites to create a perspective for the future.[cite: 1]
